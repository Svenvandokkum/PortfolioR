Projects

In this section you can look at the projects I am most proud of. Most of these projects were a result of a university projects but some were made during my freetime.

July 2025

Block detection and classification

As part of a large project with a group of 15 we had an ambitious goal of creating a product where users can 'build' their own city, with children's building blocks as input. I was responsible for making AI which detected and classified the blocks.

April 2025

Flowey

Meet Flowey! He is a speech-activated candy dispenser with sass. I made him for a project together with a duo. When you ask him for a candy very nicely he will give you one, if you're sad he'll even give one extra. But if you ask for something else, he won't. And if you ask angrily he will look at you even angrier!

November 2024

Smart Technology Car

As part of a hackathon we made a Smart-Tech Car. It's goal was to navigate a playing field and find bar-codes, and to stop at the correct bar-code. To know at which bar-code to stop, it had to demodulate an AM signal. I did the demodulation.

Project Euleur

Project Euler is an online platform that offers a series of challenging mathematical and computational problems. Typically, solving an Project Euleur problem requires a mix of mathematical insight and efficient programming. Over the course of some months I have solved over 50 of these problems.

Hive

Hive is a board-game similar to chess, but more complex. As a competition between some friends, I am programming Hive in python and intend to make an AI that can play Hive after I am done progrmamming the game. Currently the game is roughly 70% complete and I hope to finish soon!
